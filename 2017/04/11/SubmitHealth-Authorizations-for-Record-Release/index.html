How This Helps

Our record request service is one of the first ideas my co-founder, Kevin, approached me with when we founded SubmitHealth.

One of the frustrations he had encountered at his practice was the manual and slow nature of the record request process. He, like many other providers, has a patient portal, but had found it generally useless as many requesters are not very tech-savy, and don’t want to seek out a patient portal, nor do they want to navigate a non-user friendly environment. Hence, the process had reverted to the typical horrendous use of phone calls and faxes.

So, here was the general and painful workflow:

  1. Patient or patient representative calls to request a record
  2. Practice directs them to an online downloadable pdf authorization form
  3. Patient or patient representative completes the form and faxes, mails, or delivers the form
  4. Practice processes the request
  5. Practice initiates follow up administrative billing for larger records that are faxed or mailed
  6. Patient or patient representative mails payment
  7. Practice deposits payment

The whole process can take several months, and often leaves all parties involved somewhat dissatisfied.

SubmitHealth provides patients the ability to authorize the release of records online

Here’s a quick demo for how this tool works for both patients and providers:

With this tool, our workflow is simplified to:

  1. Patient or patient representative finds or is directed to the online authorization form
  2. Patient or patient representative submits the online form, capturing info and credit card payment
  3. Practice processes the request

This process has helped to dramatically simplify the workflow at Kevin’s practice. Furthermore, it has proven a really great tool to work on behalf of patients to request their records in a simple and timely manner.
